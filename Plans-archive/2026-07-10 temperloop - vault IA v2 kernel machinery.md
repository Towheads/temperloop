## Problem

The vault's write half (capture → distill via `/tidy`) is heavily engineered, but the read half was never built: nothing records which notes are ever loaded into context, nothing routes a pattern to the moment its trigger fires, and nothing ever schedules re-reading — so we cannot distinguish a compounding knowledge base from an expensive write-only archive, and structural drift (junk-drawer folders, naming drift, stale plans) accumulates unlinted. The locked ADR ([[Decisions/foundation - Vault information architecture v2 (ADR)]]) resolves the design; this plan executes its **kernel machinery** (Phases 0/2/4 + the kernel slices of Phase 1). The vault content moves themselves (Phases 1/3) are overlay-supervised sessions, deliberately outside this plan — every kernel item ships path fallbacks so nothing assumes the new folders exist yet.
